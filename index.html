Why a grade conversion calculator built for international students?

Most online grade calculators were built for US students. They show a single number — often wrong — and never explain how confident they are in that number. For international students applying to US or UK graduate programs, this false precision can be genuinely harmful: you might decide not to apply to a program you could get into, or apply to one that's out of reach.

GradeCalc is different in one fundamental way: every result shows its confidence tier — High, Moderate, or Estimate — so you know when to trust the number and when to verify it. If an official university formula exists (like VTU's or Anna University's), we use it and label the result High Confidence. If it's a widely-used approximation (like UK degree to US GPA), we show a range with Moderate confidence. If there's no standard at all, we say so explicitly.

What the confidence tiers mean

High Confidence means an official formula exists, published by the university or institution. The result is exact. Moderate Confidence means a widely-accepted approximation is used, but the result depends on the receiving institution. We show a range. Estimate means there is no official standard — the result is for general reference only, and you should verify directly with your target institution.

No other grade calculator in this space labels its results this way. It's a trust feature, not a cop-out.

Who this site is for

Primarily Indian undergraduate students applying to MS programs in the US or UK. Indian universities use 10-point CGPA scales, percentages, or letter grades — none of which map cleanly to the 4.0 US GPA scale. The conversion is genuinely ambiguous, and the AI Advisor on this site helps explain what your specific grade means for specific programs.

These calculators are for informational purposes only. Always verify conversions directly with your target institution or a credential evaluator such as WES before submitting applications.
