What is WES and why does it matter?

World Education Services (WES) is a non-profit organization that evaluates international academic credentials for use in the United States and Canada. Founded in 1974, WES is one of the most widely recognized and trusted credential evaluation services in North America. When you apply to a US or Canadian graduate program, employer, or immigration authority, they may require a WES evaluation to verify and contextualize your international degree.

WES doesn't just confirm that your degree is legitimate — it translates your academic record into a format US and Canadian institutions understand. This means converting your Indian grading system (whether CGPA or percentage) into an equivalent US GPA on a 4.0 scale, which is universally used by American and Canadian universities for admission decisions, scholarship eligibility, and academic standing assessments.

The WES iGPA: what it is and how it works

The WES iGPA (International GPA) is the GPA that WES assigns to your international academic record after evaluation. It is calculated using WES's proprietary methodology, which maps each grade from your transcript to an equivalent US grade point, then computes a weighted average based on credit hours. The result is a number on the 0–4.0 scale, with corresponding letter grades.

WES maintains a large database of international grading systems and updates their conversion methodology regularly. For Indian universities, WES uses a combination of percentage bands and CGPA scales depending on the institution, because different Indian universities have different grading philosophies. A 7.5 CGPA at one university does not necessarily represent the same academic achievement as 7.5 at another — WES attempts to account for these institutional differences, though the exact methodology is not fully public.

WES iGPA methodology for Indian universities

For Indian universities, WES evaluations generally follow these broad conversion bands (approximate, Moderate Confidence):

  • CGPA 9.5–10.0: Typically maps to 4.0 (A) on the US scale
  • CGPA 8.5–9.4: Typically maps to 3.7 (A-)
  • CGPA 7.5–8.4: Typically maps to 3.3 (B+)
  • CGPA 6.5–7.4: Typically maps to 3.0 (B)
  • CGPA 5.5–6.4: Typically maps to 2.7 (B-)
  • CGPA below 5.5: Typically maps to 2.3 or lower

These bands are approximations based on publicly documented WES methodology and community-reported evaluation results. Your actual WES evaluation may differ. The WES iGPA Calculator tool on the WES website itself acknowledges that the result is an estimate.

CGPA vs. percentage input — which should you use?

If your transcript shows a CGPA (which is typical for universities that adopted the UGC 10-point scale — including most engineering and science colleges after 2010), use the CGPA input. If your transcript shows only a percentage (common at older universities, some arts and commerce colleges, and pre-2010 transcripts), use the percentage input.

Do not convert your CGPA to percentage yourself and then enter the percentage — this introduces an extra layer of approximation. If you have a CGPA, enter it directly. If you have both on your transcript, use the CGPA input as it is generally more precise for WES conversions.

Worked examples

CGPA 7.0 → 3.3 (B+): A student from an IIT-affiliated institute with CGPA 7.0 would likely receive a WES iGPA of approximately 3.3. This is a competitive score for US master's programs in STEM, though top programs (MIT, Stanford, CMU) typically see applicants with 3.7+.

CGPA 8.5 → 3.7 (A-): A CGPA of 8.5 from most Indian engineering universities typically receives a WES iGPA of around 3.7. This is considered strong for US graduate admissions and meets the GPA requirements for most competitive programs.

65% → 3.7–4.0 range: A percentage of 65 maps to a WES iGPA range of approximately 3.7–4.0 depending on the university and grading curve. Note that 65% at one institution may represent very different academic performance than 65% at another — WES accounts for this to some extent.

Limitations of this calculator and WES estimates generally

This calculator provides an estimate based on WES's documented methodology and publicly available information. It carries Moderate Confidence — meaning it is a reasonable approximation but not a guaranteed outcome. Actual WES evaluations are affected by factors this tool cannot know: which specific university you attended, historical grade inflation or deflation at that institution, the specific courses on your transcript, and WES's internal adjustments for institutional quality.

For official applications, always obtain a real WES evaluation. Do not use this calculator's output as your official GPA on any application — it is an estimation tool for planning and self-assessment only.
