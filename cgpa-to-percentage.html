How to convert CGPA to percentage

Converting CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) to a percentage is not universal in India — different universities use different official formulas, and using the wrong one can result in a percentage that is 5–15% higher or lower than your actual equivalent. This calculator shows you all four major formulas simultaneously for any CGPA you enter.

The Indian CGPA grading system

Most Indian universities adopted a 10-point CGPA scale after the UGC (University Grants Commission) recommended it in 2010, moving away from the traditional percentage-based assessment. Under this system, each course is graded on a 10-point scale (typically with grade points like O=10, A+=9, A=8, B+=7, etc.) and the CGPA is the weighted average of all grade points earned across credit hours.

The challenge is that different universities implemented the UGC recommendation with different formulas for converting back to a percentage equivalent — because percentage is still required for job applications, graduate school applications, and credential evaluations. There is no single national standard.

Official formulas by university

Anna University (Tamil Nadu): Percentage = CGPA × 9.5. Published in an official Anna University circular and used by all affiliated colleges in Tamil Nadu. This gives a maximum of 95% for a 10 CGPA.

VTU (Visvesvaraya Technological University, Karnataka): Percentage = (CGPA − 0.75) × 10. Published in an official VTU notification. Note: this formula has a minimum valid CGPA of 1.0, and for very high CGPAs can theoretically exceed 100%.

Mumbai University: Percentage = (CGPA × 7.1) + 11. Published in University of Mumbai ordinances. This formula produces a different scale — a 10 CGPA gives 82%, not 95%.

Generic (other universities): CGPA × 9.5 or CGPA × 10. These are approximations only and should be treated as estimates, not official results.

Why conversion matters for graduate applications

When you apply to US or UK graduate programs, your application includes your Indian transcript with its CGPA. The admissions committee — or the credential evaluator (WES, ECE, etc.) they use — converts it to a GPA equivalent for comparison. Understanding what your CGPA means in percentage terms helps you gauge where you stand relative to program requirements and average admitted student profiles.

For Indian job applications, many companies still ask for percentage rather than CGPA, even for students from universities that issue only CGPA. Using your university's official formula ensures you're not misrepresenting your academic record.

Worked examples

CGPA 6.0: Anna = 57%, VTU = 52.5%, Mumbai = 53.6%

CGPA 7.5: Anna = 71.25%, VTU = 67.5%, Mumbai = 64.25%

CGPA 9.0: Anna = 85.5%, VTU = 82.5%, Mumbai = 74.9%
