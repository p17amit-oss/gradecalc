What is the grade needed calculator?

This calculator answers one of the most common academic questions: "What do I need to score on my final exam (or remaining assessments) to end up with a specific overall grade?" It uses an exact arithmetic formula and gives you a clear, honest answer — including telling you clearly when the target is no longer possible.

Understanding weighted grade calculations

Most courses assign different weights to different assessments. A typical course might have: quizzes (10%), assignments (20%), midterm exam (30%), and final exam (40%). If you've completed the quizzes, assignments, and midterm, you've completed 60% of the course. The grade you earned on that 60% is your current grade for this calculation.

The remaining 40% (the final exam) is what you control next. The calculator finds the minimum score needed on that 40% to achieve your target overall grade.

The formula explained

Required = (Target − Current × WeightCompleted) ÷ WeightRemaining

All weight values are expressed as decimals (60% = 0.60). For example: current grade 70%, 70% complete, target 80%: Required = (80 − 70 × 0.70) ÷ 0.30 = (80 − 49) ÷ 0.30 = 103.3% — which is impossible.

The "impossible" state

This calculator will tell you clearly when a target is mathematically impossible, rather than showing a nonsensical number like "103%" without context. When your required score exceeds 100%, your options are to lower your target grade, speak with your instructor about extra credit or resubmission opportunities, or plan to retake the course if it's required for graduation or GPA maintenance.

Worked examples

Example 1: Current 85%, 60% done, target 80%: Required = (80 − 85×0.6) ÷ 0.4 = (80 − 51) ÷ 0.4 = 72.5% → Achievable.

Example 2: Current 90%, 80% done, target 85%: Required = (85 − 90×0.8) ÷ 0.2 = (85 − 72) ÷ 0.2 = 65% → Achievable with room to spare.

Example 3: Current 70%, 70% done, target 80%: Required = (80 − 70×0.7) ÷ 0.3 = 103.3% → Mathematically impossible.

Tips for grade planning

Use this calculator at the start of each assessment period, not just before finals. Knowing early that a target is slipping gives you time to course-correct. For graduate school applications, aim for B+ (87%) or above in core courses — admissions committees pay attention to grade trends within your transcript, not just your overall GPA.
