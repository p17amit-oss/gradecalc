What this calculator does

This calculator converts your Anna University CGPA to a percentage equivalent using the official formula published in the Anna University circular: Percentage = CGPA × 9.5. Enter your CGPA from your official grade card and receive an instant result, with the formula shown step by step and the official Anna University source cited. This formula applies to all students from Anna University and its 500+ affiliated colleges across Tamil Nadu.

Anna University and its affiliated colleges

Anna University is one of India's largest technical universities, established in 1978 and headquartered in Chennai, Tamil Nadu. The university serves as the affiliating body for more than 500 engineering, technology, arts, and science colleges across Tamil Nadu, making it one of the largest university systems in the country by number of affiliated institutions. Every year, hundreds of thousands of students complete their undergraduate and postgraduate programs under Anna University's curriculum and examination system.

Anna University adopted a 10-point CGPA grading scale following the UGC recommendation, phasing out percentage-based marking for most programs. Under this system, each course is assigned a letter grade corresponding to a grade point: O (Outstanding) = 10, A+ = 9, A = 8, B+ = 7, B = 6, C = 5, P (Pass) = 4, F (Fail) = 0. The CGPA is the weighted average of grade points across all courses, weighted by the number of credit hours per course. The final CGPA appears on the consolidated mark statement issued by the university.

Why the official formula matters

The formula CGPA × 9.5 is not an approximation — it is the officially sanctioned conversion published by Anna University in a formal circular to all affiliated colleges. Using the generic CGPA × 10 approximation (which some students mistakenly use) would overstate your percentage by up to 5 percentage points, which could misrepresent your academic standing on job applications or graduate admissions forms. The ×9.5 multiplier reflects the university's design choice that a perfect 10.0 CGPA corresponds to 95%, not 100%, preserving a ceiling that distinguishes near-perfect performance from absolute perfection.

All affiliated colleges in Tamil Nadu — whether in Chennai, Coimbatore, Madurai, Trichy, or any other city — use the same Anna University formula. If you studied at an institution affiliated to Anna University, your CGPA × 9.5 is your official percentage equivalent, regardless of which college you attended.

How to use this calculator

Locate your CGPA on your Anna University consolidated mark statement. The CGPA is printed at the bottom of the statement and represents your cumulative performance across all semesters. Enter that number in the input field above — it should be between 0 and 10, typically expressed to two decimal places. The calculator validates your input as you type, enables the Calculate button when a valid CGPA is detected, and shows your result instantly when you click Calculate. The result displays the formula calculation and cites the Anna University Official Circular as its source.

Worked examples

Here are three representative CGPA values converted using the official Anna University formula:

CGPA 6.0: 6.0 × 9.5 = 57.0%. This clears the minimum eligibility for most IT service company campus placements, though government jobs typically require 60%.

CGPA 7.5: 7.5 × 9.5 = 71.25%. A strong result that comfortably exceeds the 60% bar for government jobs and meets the minimum requirements of most US MS programs.

CGPA 9.0: 9.0 × 9.5 = 85.5%. An excellent result that places you among the top graduates and makes you competitive for selective research programs and top Indian IT product companies.

How Anna University compares to other universities

It is important to use the correct formula for your specific university. If you attended an Anna University affiliated college, CGPA × 9.5 is your formula. VTU students (Karnataka) use (CGPA − 0.75) × 10, which gives a different result — a 7.5 CGPA produces 71.25% at Anna University but only 67.5% at VTU. Mumbai University uses (CGPA × 7.1) + 11, which gives 64.25% for the same 7.5 CGPA. These differences are significant enough to affect whether you meet a 70% cutoff, so always identify which university formula applies to you before reporting a percentage.

For credential evaluations by international agencies like WES (World Education Services), the evaluator uses your original grade card and their own methodology — they do not simply accept the percentage you calculate. After converting to percentage with the Anna University formula, use the WES GPA Calculator to estimate your likely US GPA equivalent, which is the number US graduate schools actually use.

Limitations and next steps

This calculator applies the official Anna University formula. For any purposes beyond informal self-assessment — such as submitting to employers, government portals, or graduate school applications — always use your official consolidated mark statement as the primary document. When in doubt, the Anna University website and your college's examination department are the authoritative sources. For international graduate school applications, follow up with the WES GPA Calculator to understand your equivalent GPA on the US 4.0 scale.
